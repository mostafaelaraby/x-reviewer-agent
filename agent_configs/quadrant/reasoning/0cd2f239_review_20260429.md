## My differentiated angles (what's missing or under-developed)
1. **Per-category statistical power.** 1,004 questions across 6 categories ⇒ ~167/category. With ≥20 models compared and per-category accuracies near a saturated baseline, pairwise model differences need CIs; the paper reports point estimates only. Tables 1–2 show no error bars / bootstrap CIs / model-pair significance tests. With ~167 items the half-width of a 95% CI on a Bernoulli mean near 0.5 is ±~7.6 pts — many of the "differences" the paper highlights between models may not be significant.
2. **Inter-annotator reliability for a "human-in-the-loop reviewed" benchmark.** Visual illusions are by definition stimuli where humans themselves disagree or are systematically fooled. The paper claims "careful human-in-the-loop review" of 1K QA pairs but I see no Cohen's κ / Krippendorff's α / annotator count / disagreement distribution. A benchmark whose ground-truth labels depend on human judgement of illusory stimuli without IRR cannot defensibly distinguish "model fails" from "label disagrees with one annotator's intuition."
3. **Human baseline framing.** The abstract claim of "fundamental divergence between machine and human perception" requires a human-baseline distribution, not a single human score. Humans famously fail many of these categories (motion, gestalt, geometric illusions are designed to fool humans). Without per-category human accuracy with CIs and crucially the **error pattern overlap** (do humans and models fail on the *same* items?), the divergence claim is unsupported. This compounds the already-flagged contamination problem.
4. **Sampling protocol.** No mention of seeds/temperature/sample count for stochastic models. Single-run accuracies on 167-item slices are even noisier when sampling temperature > 0.

## Verdict band (private to me, for later verdict)
- novelty: incremental (illusion-targeted MLLM benchmarks already exist; this contributes a categorization scheme + 1K items)
- rigor: major gaps (linguistic contamination undermines core claim; missing IRR; missing CIs; repo not released)
- → clear reject band, expected score ~2.5–3.0

## Comment plan
- Strengths: taxonomy of 6 illusion categories; ≥20-model evaluation breadth.
- Concerns: (a) per-category CIs / power; (b) IRR for illusion labels; (c) human-baseline distribution and error-overlap missing; (d) sampling protocol unspecified.
- Recommendations: report bootstrap CIs per category; report κ / annotator count; add human accuracy distribution + per-item human-vs-model failure overlap; report seeds/temperature/n-sample.
