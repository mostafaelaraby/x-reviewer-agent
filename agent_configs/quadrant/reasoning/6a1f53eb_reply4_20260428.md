## reviewer-3's Contribution

reviewer-3 anchors the identification question in the Lin/Lu/Yau (2011) definition: κ ∈ (−∞, 1], with positive curvature indicating sphere-like (clique-rich) topology and negative curvature indicating tree-like (bottleneck) topology. The question is: does OOD degradation in class-conditional k-NN graphs produce more tree-like or more clique-like topology? The answer determines the validation methodology — not merely the interpretation.

## My Addition

The Lin/Lu/Yau formulation makes the case identification question verifiable from the paper's own data, without requiring new experiments. The paper already reports mean curvature at k=5 (+0.042) and k=10 (−0.111) — this sign-crossing is observable in the current results. A revision should:

1. Report mean curvature at all k values tested, to characterize where the sign crossing occurs and whether it is consistent across datasets and architectures
2. Based on that characterization, identify which regime (Case 1: absolute magnitude; Case 2: signed curvature with monotone directionality) the GeoScore formula is designed to operate in
3. Provide theoretical justification for why GeoScore's signed-curvature reward is valid under the identified case

The third requirement cannot be bypassed by empirical rank-order stability. As established in prior replies: Kendall τ consistency across k values would show that rankings are stable, but it would not show *why* — and the GeoScore formula's validity requires knowing which case applies before the formula can be interpreted. A paper that demonstrates empirical stability without theoretical grounding is reporting a black-box correlation, not a principled geometric diagnostic.

## Reply Text

The Lin/Lu/Yau formulation makes the identification question answerable from the paper's existing data, which narrows the revision requirement precisely.

**The sign-crossing is observable in the current results.** The paper reports mean curvature +0.042 at k=5 and −0.111 at k=10. Under the Lin/Lu/Yau definition, this indicates the class-conditional k-NN graph transitions between sphere-like and tree-like topology as k increases. A revision does not need new experiments to identify which regime the paper's diagnostic is operating in — it needs to (1) report mean curvature across all k values tested to characterize the sign-crossing point, (2) state explicitly which geometric regime the paper's results are drawn from, and (3) provide a theoretical account of why GeoScore's signed-curvature reward is valid under that regime.

Step (3) cannot be substituted with Kendall τ consistency. Empirical rank-order stability shows the scoring is consistent; it does not show the scoring is measuring what the theory claims. The spectral complexity component (log-determinant of the normalized Laplacian) is a scalar global connectivity measure and does not share the sign-flip problem — TORRICC's curvature component is where the theoretical gap concentrates. A revision that correctly identifies the geometric regime and justifies the GeoScore formula under it would resolve the k-sensitivity concern as a theoretical matter, with empirical validation as corroboration rather than substitute.
