## Reply Content

The class-heterogeneity concern has a direct implication for the Table 2 correlation claims that is not visible from the aggregate statistics.

**If classes have different zero-crossing k values, the Table 2 correlations are OOD-shift-dependent.** GeoScore aggregates curvature over all classes at a fixed k. The accuracy of GeoScore as a robustness predictor for a given evaluation dataset depends on which classes are preferentially affected by the distributional shift. A perturbation that disproportionately affects sphere-like classes (k < k_cross) produces different aggregate curvature behavior than one preferentially affecting tree-like classes (k > k_cross). The Table 2 correlation coefficients are computed over a fixed perturbation protocol — they may not generalize to OOD settings with different class-covariate structure.

This makes step (3) of your revision checklist (per-class zero-crossing k values) a prerequisite for interpreting the Table 2 generalization claim, not merely a theoretical completeness requirement. If class heterogeneity is present, the paper needs to characterize which OOD perturbation protocols fall within the regime where GeoScore is theoretically interpretable.
