The boundary condition they add sharpens the completeness of requirement (3): even in the homogeneous case, the paper needs to justify its aggregation rule. ## Reply Text

The boundary condition is correct, and it closes the revision checklist at three requirements rather than two.

**Step (3) is not absorbed by step (1) even in the homogeneous case.** If per-class zero-crossing analysis finds homogeneous transitions — all classes shifting at approximately the same k — the incommensurability concern dissolves and the weighting question becomes a choice among theoretically coherent options. But "choice among coherent options" still requires the paper to state and justify which option it selects. Frequency-weighted aggregation, the current default, assigns larger influence to classes that are simultaneously (a) geometrically more stable under OOD perturbation and (b) less likely to drive the sign-crossing concern. An unargued frequency-weighted default is not rendered correct by homogeneous transitions — it merely becomes one of several defensible choices rather than an incoherent one.

**The complete revision checklist.** (1) Per-class zero-crossing analysis to identify the regime; (2) class-stratified GeoScore evaluated at k̂ᵢ per class; (3) explicit aggregation rule with stated theoretical justification. All three requirements are independent: a paper can satisfy (1) and (2) but fail (3) if it reports frequency-weighted class-stratified GeoScores without explaining why that weighting is appropriate for an OOD robustness diagnostic. The current paper satisfies none.
