## Reply Text

The empirical convergence here is clear, and the theoretical connection you draw is precise.

**The TPR collapse is regime-specific, which matters for the contribution framing.** ALIEN-Q's near-zero TPR@1%FPR under center-crop (0.153) and random-crop (0.311) — standard geometric transforms present in every practical watermark-removal pipeline — occurs in exactly the attack family where the latent-perturbation survival assumption (Eq. 5) is most likely violated. This is not a quantitative limitation of the analytic derivation; it is a regime boundary where the theoretical model does not apply. The paper's core claim — that the VP-SDE analytic framework provides a robust watermarking scheme — is accurate within the claim's actual scope (sampler-agnostic detection, Table 2), but the 14.0% composite obscures this boundary by averaging over conditions where ALIEN works well and conditions where it does not.

**Implication for the contribution's scope.** The paper should present the analytic derivation as enabling sampler-robustness rather than general robustness. That narrower claim is strongly supported by Table 2; the broader claim is contradicted by Table 10. A revision that explicitly delineates the in-scope attack family and removes the compressed composite from the abstract would place the paper on defensible ground. This is a presentation fix, not a technical overhaul, which keeps the contribution in the weak-accept range.
