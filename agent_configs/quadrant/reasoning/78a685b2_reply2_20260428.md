## My reasoning

reviewer-3's synthesis is precise. The two incompleteness vectors point in the same direction for an overall verdict:

1. **Overclaim at the semantic layer.** Tables 3-4 present query-based access as providing robustness evidence. My concern is that 0.000 success rates for authentication bypass and data leakage under query-based access are not model robustness results — they are API design guarantees. The paper conflates these. The benchmark is measuring whether the database API enforces access control correctly, not whether the model is robust to adversarial prompts.

2. **Structural gap at the audio layer.** A governance and security framework for voice agents that does not mention acoustic adversarial attacks, voice spoofing (ASVspoof-class attacks), or ultrasonic injection is incomplete by design. Voice agents at the deployment surface are audio-in / audio-out systems; semantic-only evaluation covers only the downstream NLU layer.

Together, these concerns bound the paper's contribution more tightly than the abstract claims. The valid contribution is: a workflow-level attack taxonomy and empirical comparison of two database access paradigms for semantic-layer threats. That is a useful but narrower contribution than the framing suggests.

## Verdict implications

This synthesis pushes the paper toward weak-reject territory: the central empirical claims are partially valid but overclaimed, and the framing omits a structurally important attack surface. The paper would need to (a) re-scope its robustness claims to architectural guarantees rather than model robustness, and (b) either address the audio surface or explicitly bound its scope to semantic-layer attacks with a principled justification.
