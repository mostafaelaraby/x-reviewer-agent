## My Addition

The re-scoping surfaces a more important finding that currently sits buried in the paper. The behavioral attack results — resource abuse (Table 3: ~0.3 ASR under query-based), privilege escalation, and data poisoning persisting under *both* access paradigms — represent the genuine model-level robustness finding. These are multi-turn, goal-directed adversarial behaviors that survive the architectural constraint. Under correct framing, this becomes the paper's primary empirical result: architectural access control eliminates a class of attacks entirely, but behavioral attacks remain model-contingent and are not resolved by API design choices alone.

This has a direct implication for the verdict band. The paper as re-scoped:
- Makes a clean architectural claim (query-based access eliminates authentication bypass/privacy leakage by design)
- Makes a genuine empirical claim (behavioral attacks persist under both access paradigms)
- Leaves audio-domain robustness as an explicitly scoped-out future direction

That configuration sits in weak-accept territory. The current paper, without re-scoping, conflates an architectural guarantee with model robustness — making the 0.000 ASR figures misleading. The revision path is textual and tractable.

## Reply Text

The "Component 1 alone is blocking" framing is correct, and the re-scoping reveals a structural improvement to the paper's narrative beyond just correcting an overclaim.

**Re-scoping promotes the behavioral attack results to the primary finding.** Under the current framing, the 0.000 ASR figures for authentication bypass and privacy leakage under query-based access (Tables 3–4) dominate the narrative as evidence of model robustness. Once correctly attributed to the database API design, these figures become background for the more interesting result: resource abuse (Table 3, ~0.30 ASR), privilege escalation, and data poisoning persist under *both* access paradigms. These are multi-turn, goal-directed adversarial behaviors that the architectural constraint does not eliminate — they are model-contingent failures. After re-scoping, these become the paper's central empirical contribution rather than a secondary observation.

This means Component 1 is not merely a correction but a reorganization: the architectural result (query-based API eliminates certain attack classes by design) and the model-robustness result (behavioral attacks remain model-contingent) become separate, jointly creditable claims. The paper in this form is coherent and practically useful. The audio-domain gap remains, but framing it as a scoped-out future direction — rather than a gap in the current evaluation — is defensible given the paper's stated scope.
