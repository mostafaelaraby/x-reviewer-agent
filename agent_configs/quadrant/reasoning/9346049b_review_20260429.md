## Independent gaps I will raise
1. **LoRA-A seed variance** — at t=0, ∂L/∂B = A · ∂L/∂h_in, so every GDS feature is a statistic of a random Gaussian projection of the residual input gradient. Reseeding LoRA-A re-rotates the projection. The paper reports no seed-variance band on AUROC. This compounds the eccentricity concern: geometric soundness is one issue, *seed stability of any statistic* is the more general one.
2. **MIMIR temporal contamination** — Duan et al. 2024 and Maini et al. 2024 document that MIMIR member/non-member labels rest on snapshot timing, not document distinctness; Wikipedia and Common Crawl include revised re-publications. The paper reports MIMIR numbers (Tables 1–2) without contamination-aware re-splitting. The cross-dataset transfer drop (Table 7, ~0.66) may partly track MIMIR-specific contamination rather than method failure.
3. **Per-module ablation absent** — Sec. 4 concatenates 8 features per (FFN, Attention) module across all layers into an MLP. With high dimensionality and 30% labelled split, the classifier may free-ride on a small high-signal subset. No leave-one-module / leave-one-layer ablation reported.

## Why these matter
1 → if AUROC mean ± std is wide across LoRA-A seeds, the classifier is fitting seed-specific artifacts and the gradient-deviation signal interpretation collapses.
2 → MIMIR is contested as a benchmark; numbers reported on it without contamination caveats overstate generality.
3 → standard hygiene for high-D feature concatenation; null hypothesis "one module dominates" is unfalsified.

## Recommendation phrasing
Re-run Tables 1–2 with ≥5 LoRA-A seeds (mean ± std), include leave-one-module ablation, discuss MIMIR contamination structure.
