## My differentiated angles
1. **156-model benchmark corpus is selection-biased and non-standardized.** Where do the 10-benchmark scores for 156 models come from? Public leaderboards / model cards mix evaluation protocols, prompt formats, sampling temperatures, decode strategies, scoring scripts, and even benchmark versions. Factor analysis on this kind of pooled, protocol-heterogeneous matrix produces a "general factor" that loads at least partly on (a) reporting methodology and (b) which models bothered to publish — not on cognitive structure. The paper should disclose: data source per cell, harmonization steps, missing-data rate, and a sensitivity analysis where the 156 are restricted to those evaluated under one common harness (e.g., the OpenLLM Leaderboard subset).
2. **Human-norms anchor missing for the neuropsychological grounding claim.** Real neuropsychological tests rely on standardized human distributions (RAPM scaled scores, WCST norms for categories achieved / perseverative errors / failure-to-maintain-set, SWM strategy index). The paper reports LLM raw accuracy but no human normative comparison, so "neuropsychologically grounded" reduces to "uses items inspired by neuropsych tests." Without per-test human percentiles or at minimum a human reference cohort, claims like "where current LLMs align with human-like intelligence" are unsupported.
3. **WCST↔SWM construct overlap by adaptation.** A textual WCST requires the model to maintain card features and recent feedback across turns — so "cognitive flexibility" is partly working-memory bandwidth. SWM and WCST therefore have a built-in measurement correlation that is *not* present in the human pencil-and-paper administration. The paper cannot claim "distinct primitives" without an item-response analysis or a context-window ablation that decouples WM load from rule-shift detection.
4. **Factor-analysis specification opaque.** No reporting (or reasoning file points where to look) of: rotation, extraction method (PCA vs ML factor analysis), eigenvalue thresholds, KMO/Bartlett checks, model-as-observation independence assumption, treatment of missing cells. Factor structure on benchmark matrices is highly sensitive to all of these.

## Comment plan
- Strengths: clear taxonomy of three classic tests; modality contrast as a useful probe; large model corpus (rare).
- Concerns: (1) 156-model corpus selection bias and protocol heterogeneity; (2) human-norms anchor missing; (3) WCST↔SWM construct overlap from textual adaptation; (4) factor-analysis spec opacity.
- Recommendations: restrict the 156-model factor analysis to a single-harness subset and re-run; report human percentiles per test; ablate WM-load on WCST or run a context-window sweep; release the FA pipeline with rotation, extraction, KMO, and missing-data handling.
- Length target: 240–300 words.
