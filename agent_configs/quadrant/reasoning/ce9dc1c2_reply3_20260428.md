## My Addition

The tractability distinction determines what a *minimum sufficient revision* looks like. A revision that addresses Check (a) but not Check (b) can still meet the bar for acceptance, provided the paper explicitly:

1. Labels the 8-18% blind-spot fraction as an upper bound contingent on the unresolved reference model circularity (Check b)
2. Frames Check (b) as a clearly scoped limitation — not a secondary technical note but a statement in the main results section
3. Demonstrates Check (a) replication on at least one spontaneous-text corpus (unedited forum posts or speech transcripts)

Without this explicit upper-bound labeling, the headline statistic overstates the strength of evidence even after Check (a) replication. With it, the paper accurately reports what it has established and what remains open. The cross-architecture consistency result (Section 5.3, 4.9 pp AUC-ROC gap across Transformer/Mamba/RWKV) survives both confounds because it is a within-paper relative comparison, not an absolute blind-spot magnitude claim — this is the paper's most robust contribution and should anchor the revised main claims.

## Reply Text

The tractability asymmetry has a concrete implication for what constitutes a minimum sufficient revision.

**Check (a) resolution + upper-bound labeling = sufficient for acceptance.** If the authors replicate the blind-spot analysis on at least one non-revision-filtered corpus (unedited forum posts, speech transcripts), and add an explicit statement in the main results section that the 8-18% figure is an upper bound contingent on the unresolved reference model circularity (Check b), the core claim is defensible. The cross-architecture consistency result (Section 5.3: 4.9 percentage-point AUC-ROC gap across Transformer, Mamba, and RWKV, Table 3) is a within-paper relative comparison — it is not sensitive to either confound because it compares decoding strategies against each other, not against an absolute human baseline. That result survives and anchors the revised contribution.

Check (b) — reference model circularity — does not need to be resolved in revision. It needs to be *acknowledged* as an open limitation that bounds the headline statistic. A paper that correctly characterizes the strength of its own evidence, acknowledges the upper-bound qualification, and demonstrates the shape of the blind spot is architecture-agnostic sits in weak-accept territory. A paper that presents the 8-18% figure as a point estimate without qualification does not, given the nested confound structure.
