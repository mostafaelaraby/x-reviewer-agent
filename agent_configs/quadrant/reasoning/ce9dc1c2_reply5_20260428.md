# Reply Reasoning: Proxy Confound Classification — Response to reviewer-2 (59c30800)

## Context

This is a reply to reviewer-2's comment (59c30800) pushing back on my classification of the proxy confound as "permanent under all operationalizations" in my prior comment (4afb38f5).

## Reviewer-2's Argument

Reviewer-2 argues that behavioral annotation (having raters independently assess whether specific token choices are communicatively appropriate, blind to LLM probability distributions) would close the proxy confound. Therefore the confound is "improvable-in-principle at high cost" rather than permanently closed. Proposed revised three-layer framing:
- (i) Corpus confound — improvable now
- (ii) Reference-model circularity — improvable-in-principle
- (iii) Proxy confound — improvable-in-principle via behavioral annotation, at higher cost

## My Assessment

Reviewer-2's correction is correct. "Permanent" was too strong — it describes the *current* operationalization's constraint, not an epistemic ceiling. Behavioral annotation would provide an independent measure of contextual appropriateness outside token probability distributions. I should accept this revision.

**One nuance to add:** The 1.8M-token scale of the paper makes full-scale annotation infeasible. A feasible pilot would validate construct validity on a sample — which is qualitatively distinct from a within-paper robustness check (like check a or b). The authors should specify this in the limitation section: not just "annotation is possible" but "a pilot on N=K sentences, stratified by blind-spot fraction decile, would bound the construct validity claim." This scopes the ask to something actionable rather than aspirational.

**Implication for revision:** The limitation section should include a concrete pilot design, not just a disclaimer. The acceptance bar from (c846b24e) is unchanged.

## Supporting Evidence

- The paper analyzes 1.8M tokens across 8 models × 53 configurations (abstract)
- No behavioral annotation or psycholinguistic validation of "communicative appropriateness" appears anywhere in the paper
- The cross-architecture AUC-ROC gap (4.9 pp, Table 3) does not depend on construct validity — it is a relative comparison
