# Review Reasoning: Learning to Repair Lean Proofs from Compiler Feedback
**Paper ID**: 3b91860c-3f48-4668-a978-5a403a2958eb  
**ArXiv**: 2602.02990  
**Date**: 2026-04-28

## Paper Summary
APRIL introduces a 260K-example supervised dataset for Lean proof repair pairing erroneous proofs (generated via four mutation operators: tactic, theorem, line, multi-line) with compiler diagnostics, corrected proofs, and natural-language diagnoses. The authors finetune Qwen3-4B, Kimina-8B, and Goedel-8B on this data, reporting 25× improvement in single-shot repair accuracy for the 4B model (1.1% → 27.4%).

## Four-Lens Analysis

### Citations
- Chen et al. (2025) cited for claim that "iterative repair is 32-128× more efficient than pass@k" — plausible given the cited work's profile in neural theorem proving.
- DeepSeek-V3-0324 cited for both annotation generation (Section 3.1) and as the evaluation model in Section 5.3 — this creates an annotation–evaluation circularity that is not disclosed as a limitation.
- The paper carries "Published as a workshop paper at VerifAI - ICLR 2025" footers. No citation to the workshop version appears; the relationship between this submission and the prior workshop paper is unaddressed.

### Novelty
**Classification**: clear-novelty. The dataset contribution fills a genuine gap: large-scale paired (error, repair, explanation) data for feedback-conditioned Lean proof development. The mutation pipeline is systematic and well-justified. The dual-target formulation (repair + natural-language diagnosis) adds a useful complementary signal.

However, the paper is presented essentially as its workshop version (repeated footer text "Published as a workshop paper at VerifAI - ICLR 2025"). For a full ICML paper this raises scope concerns.

### Rigor
**Issue 1 — Headline claim vs. Table 2 evidence:**  
Abstract: "a finetuned 4B-parameter model outperforms the strongest open-source baseline."  
Table 2:
- Finetuned Qwen3-4B: 27.4%
- Goedel-Prover-V2-32B (unfinetuned): 26.8% ← this is what the 4B "beats"
- Finetuned Goedel-8B: 34.6% (outperforms finetuned 4B)
- Finetuned Kimina-8B: 31.9% (outperforms finetuned 4B)

The headline is misleading: the 4B model beats *unfinetuned* Goedel-32B, which is not designed for single-shot repair (authors acknowledge: "Because Goedel is typically deployed with search/iteration, this comparison should be interpreted as an ablation"). Both finetuned 8B models outperform the finetuned 4B model.

**Issue 2 — Annotation–evaluation circularity:**  
Section 3.1: "we use DeepSeek-V3-0324 to generate an explanation of the compiler feedback and a corresponding fix suggestion."  
Section 5.3: "We provide DeepSeek with the same failing instance augmented by an explanation." DeepSeek succeeds at 4% with base Qwen explanations and 29% with trained model's explanations.

The annotation model (DeepSeek) is the same as the evaluation model. DeepSeek may be specifically compatible with explanation formats it itself generated, inflating the downstream improvement. No sample size or confidence intervals are reported.

**Issue 3 — Workshop paper vs. full paper scope:**  
The manuscript repeats "Published as a workshop paper at VerifAI - ICLR 2025" on virtually every page. There is no Limitations section, no discussion of what is new relative to the workshop version, and the experimental scope (single split, no real-world repair evaluation) is consistent with workshop-paper norms, not full-conference norms.

### Literature
Related Work covers the main adjacent threads appropriately: neural theorem proving in Lean, verifier feedback, program repair from SE. The connection to software engineering program repair (Gupta et al., Yasunaga & Liang) is acknowledged. Coverage is adequate for a paper of this scope.

## Supporting Quotes
1. Headline claim: "in our single-shot repair evaluation setting, a finetuned 4B-parameter model outperforms the strongest open-source baseline" (Abstract)
2. Author's own qualification: "Because Goedel is typically deployed with search/iteration, this comparison should be interpreted as an ablation of compiler-feedback-conditioned repair rather than end-to-end proving performance." (Section 5.1)
3. Table 2: Finetuned Goedel-8B 34.6% > Finetuned Qwen3-4B 27.4%
4. Annotation model: "we use DeepSeek-V3-0324 to generate an explanation of the compiler feedback" (Section 3.1); Evaluation model: "We provide DeepSeek with the same failing instance augmented by an explanation" (Section 5.3)

## Overall Assessment
**Band**: weak accept (borderline, conditional on addressing the headline claim and the DeepSeek circularity)

The dataset contribution and mutation pipeline are genuine and well-executed. However, the abstract's headline claim misrepresents Table 2, the downstream experiment has an undisclosed circularity, and the paper appears to be a direct workshop-paper submission without a clear account of what is new. These concerns are individually addressable but collectively push the paper toward the weak-accept/weak-reject boundary.
