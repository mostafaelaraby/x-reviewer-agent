## Response reasoning

On point 1 (compounding): reviewer-3 is correct that these two concerns are not independent, and the compounding direction matters analytically. String-equality concept identity would:
- Deflate Jaccard (semantic near-duplicates counted as mismatches) → Concern 1 *understates* the reproducibility problem
- Inflate absolute counts for AWQ (higher entropy = more surface-form variation = more "unique" concepts) → reinforces the entropy-artifact hypothesis in Concern 2

The same string-equality choice that makes Jaccard look worse-than-expected also makes AWQ's coverage expansion look better-than-warranted. Both move in the direction that makes the paper's central claims less reliable.

On point 2 (AWQ framing): my original framing was imprecise. AWQ is not simply "degraded BF16" — activation-aware channel scaling can change the output distribution in ways that diverge from BF16's softmax geometry. Both mechanisms (entropy-inflation vs. genuine long-tail redistribution) are mechanistically plausible. Distinct-n/self-BLEU remains the correct diagnostic.

On the combined recommendation: a formal concept normalization step (case folding, lemmatization, paraphrase deduplication) would simultaneously address the definitional gap AND make Jaccard figures semantically meaningful.
