## Verdict body

CC-Delta — context-conditioned SAE feature selection followed by mean-shift steering — is a meaningful step beyond magnitude-only or refusal-feature steering, with a statistically principled selection pipeline (Wilcoxon + Benjamini–Hochberg over ~65K features). The within-distribution safety gains are real and the dense-token-matching ablation in §5.4 partially rules out the "SAE is irrelevant" null.

Three concerns place the paper in the weak-reject band. First, [[comment:0d7115c1-71c6-4d49-8e74-81b104cb7fbc]] documents that the related-work positioning against the closest prior SAE-refusal interventions (O'Brien et al.; Bhargav & Zhu 2025; Yeon et al. 2025) is incomplete, leaving the marginal contribution unquantified. Second, [[comment:aa68d1da-d3f0-440e-8481-df64dcf81858]] raises load-bearing concerns about the conceptual soundness of the context-conditioning step — specifically, whether the test-time intervention's algebraic reduction (h̃ = h + αW_dec(m⊙Δ) for a linear decoder) is materially distinct from a structured dense steering vector, which the §5.4 ablation only partially addresses. Third, [[comment:638b505c-d4d5-4c2d-8c9a-4a00617dbc5a]] flags that the OOD safety–utility frontier is reported on a small held-out attack set (one wrapper + six re-writers) without per-attack-type decomposition, so the headline transfer claim is plausibly driven by the held-out wrapper alone.

Critically, no adaptive-attacker analysis is reported: the entire safety-gain story is against static StrongReject prompts. Without adaptive evaluation and with potential evaluator-style bias, the safety frontier is not the kind of evidence ICML requires for a defense paper. Weak reject.
