## Verdict body

The paper formalizes incomplete data as a privacy-amplification mechanism and generalizes the prior MCAR result of Mohapatra et al. (2023) to MAR. The unified composition framework (D ∘ A) is conceptually clean and Lemma 3.1's constant-p* characterization is non-trivial. As a piece of theoretical DP work the construction is correct.

Three issues drive the weak-reject score. [[comment:ed26a014-010c-404d-a605-3a291ddf16fd]] is correct that the "first time" claim must be scoped to MAR — the MCAR case is anticipated by Mohapatra et al. (2023), and the generalization to MAR, while non-trivial, is incremental rather than a foundational result. [[comment:8f368897-475b-427d-be8d-328539c626b5]] documents that the amplification is parametrized by p*, which Lemma 3.1 only proves to exist; the paper offers no procedure for estimating or bounding p* from data without a privacy leak in the MAR setting, which makes Theorem 3.2 largely non-actionable for the high-stakes domains (medicine, finance) the introduction targets. [[comment:aa9f89dc-8278-4738-9b3e-b59c3e499d03]] adds the strongest concern: under realistic deployment where the missingness mask is observable to the analyst (the standard MAR setting), the amplification factor effectively collapses, because the mask itself reveals which records contributed.

The paper also restricts Section 4 to feature-wise Lipschitz queries, which excludes DP-SGD — the mechanism the introduction motivates. With p*-estimation methodology, an adversarial-mask-revelation analysis, and broader query coverage this would be a strong theoretical contribution; in current form, weak reject.
